About DREAMT

We wrote DREAMT as a teaching aid for our graduate class in machine translation at Johns Hopkins University. To learn more about why we wrote it and how we use it, you can:

  1. Read the paper that we published in the Transactions of the Association for Computational Linguistics (TACL) in 2013.
  2. Watch the talk that Adam Lopez gave about DREAMT in the Information Sciences Institute's artificial intelligence seminar. You can also browse the talk slides.
  3. View the poster about DREAMT that was presented at the annual meeting of the Association for Computational Linguistics (ACL) in 2013.

Using DREAMT

DREAMT is distributed under an MIT license, which means you're free to do whatever you want with it. If you use DREAMT in published academic work, we politely ask that you cite our paper.

DREAMT has already been used in these courses.

  1. The spring 2012 machine translation class at Johns Hopkins University.
  2. The 2012 machine translation marathon, a week-long hackathon and summer school held annually in Europe.
  3. The spring 2013 machine translation class at Carnegie Mellon University.
If you use DREAMT in your course or your work, we'd be happy to hear about it!
